Course goals

Learn how to do these things:

  • Think critically about causality and experiments.
  • Use experiments to understand causal relationships and make decisions.
  • Identify and address practical complications in experiments.
  • Plan and evaluate experiments using simulations.

Experiments and causal inference are challenging.

Amgen: Of 53 "landmark" cancer studies only 6 confirmed in house.
Reproducibility Project: Cancer Biology: Replicate 50 (or 18?) experiments
  • Replicated = 5 studies
  • Replicated but inconsistencies = 4 studies
  • Did not replicate = 3 studies
  • Uninterpretable = 2 studies
"Policy: NIH plans to enhance reproducibility" Francis S. Collins & Lawrence A. Tabak, Nature (2014)
  • Poor training in experiment design
  • Inconsistent implementations
  • Focus on provocative results over technical rigor
  • Not reporting details: "blinding, randomization, replication, sample-size calculation"
  • Hiding "secret sauce" details to keep competitive advantage
